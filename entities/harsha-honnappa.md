# Harsha Honnappa

Harsha Honnappa is a researcher at Purdue University, specializing in stochastic systems, filtering theory, applied probability, and variational methods for nonlinear filtering. He works on foundational theoretical aspects of the variational approach to nonlinear filtering with correlated noise.

## 주요 기여

### The Variational Approach in Filtering and Correlated Noise (2026)
- Proved that the **Mitter–Newton variational formulation** of nonlinear filtering fails when signal and observation diffusions share a common noise source, due to mutual singularity of joint and product path measures
- Established via the **Feldman–Hájek theorem** that Gaussian measures on infinite-dimensional spaces under correlated noise are mutually singular, making the original free energy formulation invalid
- Introduced a **conditional variational principle** that replaces the prior with a reference measure preserving the noise correlation structure
- Recovered the Mitter–Newton formulation as a special case when noises are independent, and derived an explicit free energy characterization for the **linear correlated-noise setting**
- RIGOR 관련성: Provides the rigorous theoretical foundation for variational filtering with correlated noise — essential for extending RIGOR's filtering framework to systems with dependent sensor noise
