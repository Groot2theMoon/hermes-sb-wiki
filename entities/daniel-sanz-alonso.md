# Daniel Sanz-Alonso

Professor in the Department of Statistics and the Committee on Computational and Applied Mathematics at the University of Chicago. Research focuses on **data assimilation**, ensemble Kalman methods, Bayesian inverse problems, optimal transport, and scientific machine learning.

## 연구 분야
- Data assimilation and state estimation
- Ensemble Kalman methods (EnKF, EKI, autodifferentiable variants)
- Bayesian inverse problems
- Optimal transport for inference
- Uncertainty quantification
- Machine learning for PDE-constrained inverse problems

## 주요 기여
- **Inverse Problems and Data Assimilation** (Cambridge University Press, 2023) — textbook co-authored with Andrew Stuart and Armeen Taeb
- **Autodifferentiable ensemble Kalman filters** — Co-learning of states, dynamics, and filtering algorithms (with [[rebecca-willett]], SIAM MDS 2022)
- **Reduced-order autodifferentiable EnKF** — Scalable ensemble methods for high-dimensional problems (Inverse Problems, 2023)
- **CAREER award** — Ensemble Kalman Methods and Bayesian Optimization in Inverse Problems and Data Assimilation (NSF)
- **Hierarchical Bayesian inverse problems** — Analysis using high-dimensional statistics
