# Frank K. Lu

**Professor and Director of the Aerodynamics Research Center (ARC), Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, University of Texas at Arlington.** Associate Fellow of AIAA. Leading researcher in detonation-based propulsion and aerodynamic measurement techniques.

## Biography

Frank K. Lu is a professor in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at the University of Texas at Arlington (UTA), where he also directs the Aerodynamics Research Center (ARC). As an Associate Fellow of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA), he has contributed extensively to the understanding and development of detonation-based propulsion systems.

## Key Contributions

### Highly Cited Review (2011/2014)
Co-authored (with Eric M. Braun) the highly cited review **"Rotating detonation wave propulsion: Experimental challenges, modeling, and engine concepts"** — first presented at the AIAA Aerospace Sciences Meeting in 2011, and later published in the *Journal of Propulsion and Power* (2014). This review is one of the most referenced surveys of [[rotating-detonation-engine]] technology in the Western literature.

### Research Focus Areas
- **Detonation-Based Propulsion:** Comprehensive study of rotating detonation wave engines, including experimental challenges, modeling approaches, and conceptual designs
- **Aerodynamic Measurement Techniques:** Development and application of advanced instrumentation for high-speed flow diagnostics
- **Supersonic and Hypersonic Flows:** Investigation of high-speed aerodynamics relevant to advanced propulsion

### Western Perspective on RDE
Lu's work provides a critical Western complement to the Russian experimental tradition represented by [[fedor-bykovskii]] and the [[lavrentyev-institute-of-hydrodynamics]]. His review synthesizes experimental challenges and modeling approaches, identifying key pathways for practical RDE development.
