# Sergey A. Zhdan

**Professor and Head of the Heterogeneous Systems Dynamics Laboratory at Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics (LIH), Novosibirsk, Russia.** Born 1950. D.Sc. (1995). Leading scientist in the mechanics of heterogeneous reacting mixtures, modeling of explosion, and pulse and rotating detonation.

## Biography

Sergey A. Zhdan is a prominent theoretical and computational scientist in detonation physics at the Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics (LIH), Siberian Division of the Russian Academy of Sciences. With over 100 publications, he has made extensive contributions to the mathematical modeling of detonation phenomena, particularly in heterogeneous (multi-phase) reacting systems.

## Key Contributions

### Theoretical Modeling of Detonation
Zhdan's research centers on the mechanics of heterogeneous reacting mixtures, developing mathematical models for:
- Explosion dynamics in gas-liquid and gas-solid systems
- Pulse detonation processes
- Rotating detonation wave structure and stability

### Co-Author of Seminal Work
Co-authored the seminal paper **"Continuous spin detonations"** (J. Propul. Power, 2006) with [[fedor-bykovskii]]. Zhdan provided the theoretical and computational framework that complemented Bykovskii's extensive experimental program. Together, their work forms one of the most complete experimental-theoretical studies of [[rotating-detonation-engine]] physics.

### Heterogeneous Detonation Expertise
Zhdan is recognized for his expertise in:
- Gas-droplet and gas-particle detonation modeling
- Detonation initiation and propagation in heterogeneous media
- Numerical simulation of rotating detonation waves in various chamber geometries
- Shock wave interactions with multiphase media

## Collaboration

Zhdan's partnership with [[fedor-bykovskii]] at LIH represents a classic experimental-theoretical collaboration in detonation science. While Bykovskii conducted the physical experiments across diverse RDE configurations, Zhdan developed the theoretical models and numerical simulations that explained and predicted the observed phenomena.
