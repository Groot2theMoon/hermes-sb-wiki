# Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics (LIH)

**M.A. Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics, Siberian Division of the Russian Academy of Sciences, Novosibirsk, Russia.** World-leading center for detonation physics research and the birthplace of the first experimental demonstration of continuous spin detonation.

## Overview

The Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics (LIH) is a premier research institute within the Siberian Division of the Russian Academy of Sciences. Named after the renowned mathematician and physicist Mikhail Alekseevich Lavrentyev, the institute has been a global powerhouse in detonation and combustion science for over half a century.

## Historical Contributions to RDE

### First Stationary Spinning Detonation
The institute's detonation group, led originally by Voitsekhovskii, achieved the **first successful demonstration of a stationary spinning detonation wave** — the physical phenomenon that underlies the modern [[rotating-detonation-engine]]. This breakthrough laid the groundwork for all subsequent RDE research worldwide.

### Seminal Experimental Program
The comprehensive experimental program led by [[fedor-bykovskii]] at LIH systematically studied rotating detonation across:
- Multiple chamber geometries (annular, cylindrical, planar)
- Various fuel types (hydrogen, hydrocarbons, liquid fuels)
- Different oxidizers (air, oxygen, enriched air)
- Wide range of operating conditions

### Theoretical Foundation
The theoretical and computational work of [[sergey-zhdan]], head of the Heterogeneous Systems Dynamics Laboratory, provided the mathematical framework for understanding the experimental observations at LIH.

## Global Impact

LIH's research has been cited as foundational in virtually every major RDE review, including those by [[piotr-wolanski]] (Warsaw University of Technology) and [[frank-lu]] (University of Texas at Arlington). The institute's experimental data remains the benchmark for RDE modeling and design worldwide.
