# Piotr Wolański (Piotr Wolanski)

**Professor at Warsaw University of Technology, Institute of Heat Engineering.** Pioneer in detonative propulsion research and a leading figure in the development of continuous rotating detonation engines.

## Biography

Piotr Wolański (also spelled Piotr Wolanski) is a professor at the Warsaw University of Technology, Institute of Heat Engineering in Poland. He has also led Polish research on rotating detonation engines at the Institute of Aviation (Łukasiewicz Research Network). His career spans decades of fundamental and applied research in combustion and detonation physics.

## Key Contributions

### Landmark Review (2013)
Published the landmark review **"Detonative propulsion"** in *Proceedings of the Combustion Institute* (2013), which has accumulated approximately 1,200 citations. This comprehensive survey established the state of the art in detonative propulsion systems, covering both Pulse Detonation Engines (PDE) and [[rotating-detonation-engine | Rotating Detonation Engines (RDE)]].

### Detonative Propulsion Research
- **Continuous Rotating Detonation Engines (RDE):** Pioneered experimental and theoretical work on sustaining continuous detonation waves in annular and disk-shaped chambers.
- **Pulse Detonation Engines (PDE):** Contributed foundational work on pulsed detonation cycles for propulsion applications.
- Led the Polish research group at the Institute of Aviation, advancing practical RDE implementations.

### Collaboration Network
Wolański's work connects closely with the Russian school of detonation physics, including collaboration with [[fedor-bykovskii]] at the Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics. His review integrates the seminal Russian experimental findings with Western propulsion engineering perspectives.
