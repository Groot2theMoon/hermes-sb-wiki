# Fedor A. Bykovskii

**Leading Researcher at Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics (LIH), Siberian Division of Russian Academy of Sciences, Novosibirsk.** Born 1947. D.Sc. A seminal experimentalist in the physics of detonation combustion and continuous spin detonation.

## Biography

Fedor A. Bykovskii is a leading scientist at the Lavrentyev Institute of Hydrodynamics (LIH), part of the Siberian Division of the Russian Academy of Sciences in Novosibirsk, Russia. Over decades of experimental research, he has systematically studied rotating detonation across a wide range of geometries, fuels (both gaseous and liquid), and oxidizers.

## Key Contributions

### Seminal Publication (2006)
Published **"Continuous spin detonations"** in the *Journal of Propulsion and Power* (2006), which has accumulated approximately 728 citations. This paper provided a comprehensive experimental characterization of the continuous spin detonation phenomenon, establishing foundational data for the [[rotating-detonation-engine]] research community.

### Experimental Achievements
- Investigated numerous RDE geometries: annular, cylindrical, and planar configurations
- Tested a broad range of fuel types, including hydrogen, hydrocarbons, and liquid fuels
- Studied various oxidizers including air, oxygen, and oxygen-enriched air
- Characterized detonation wave stability, number of waves, and propagation modes
- Documented the transition between detonation and deflagration regimes

### Long-Running Research Program
Bykovskii's experimental program at LIH spans decades, producing a continuous stream of results that form the empirical backbone of RDE science. His work includes:

- Effects of mixture composition on detonation wave structure
- Scaling laws for RDE chambers
- Injection and mixing dynamics in rotating detonation environments

## Collaboration

Bykovskii's most notable collaborator is [[sergey-zhdan]], with whom he co-authored "Continuous spin detonations" and numerous other papers. Their combined experimental (Bykovskii) and theoretical (Zhdan) contributions have been instrumental in advancing [[rotating-detonation-engine]] research.
