# Fluid Dynamics

Fluid dynamics is the branch of physics and engineering that studies the motion of fluids (liquids and gases) and the forces acting upon them. It is governed by the Navier-Stokes equations and their simplifications, and serves as a primary application domain for physics-informed machine learning, neural operators, and data-driven modeling of complex flows.

## 개요

Fluid dynamics spans regimes from incompressible laminar flow to high-speed compressible turbulent flow, governed by conservation laws for mass, momentum, and energy. Key phenomena include boundary layers, separation, vortex dynamics, shock waves, and flow stability. Machine learning approaches — particularly Fourier Neural Operators (FNO), Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), and Koopman-based methods — are increasingly applied to surrogate modeling, flow control, reduced-order modeling, and stability analysis across aerospace, mechanical, and civil engineering applications.
