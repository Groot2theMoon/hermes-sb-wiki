**Attentional Filtering: Dynamic System State Estimation as an Attention** _[⋆]_ Andi Lin[a] ,Wen-An Zhang[a] ,Lei Guo[b] a _College of Information Engineering, Zhejiang University of Technology, Hangzhou 310023, China_ b _School of Automation Science and Electrical Engineering, Beihang University, Beijing 100191, China_ **Abstract** The ability to understand the behavior of dynamic systems is supported by state estimation techniques. Traditional methods for estimating states involve developing a probabilistic paradigm using a generic state-space representation, propagating distributions within the Bayesian framework. Due to highly nonlinear dynamics and rich sensory measurements, a fundamental bottleneck is argued to naturally emerge within Bayesian probabilistic estimators. The fundamental bottleneck stems from the intractability of exact posterior inference in complex settings. The distributions fusion for estimation purposes requires Bayesian inference based on the assumption of a specific distribution (typically Gaussian), where Bayesian inference generates only a poor approximation. To go beyond Gaussian filtering, a novel filtering framework named “Attentional Filtering” (AF) for the state estimation is framed. This framework, guided by the human attention mechanism, considers state estimation as two behavioral phenomena of sensory attention. Additionally, a specific computational model with deep learning is established for AF framework and applied to both simulation data and real-world datasets. Extensive experiments validate excellent performance and robustness of the proposed AF in complex settings. The proposed framework provides the ability to estimate states of arbitrary dynamic systems, giving potentially novel insights at state estimation. _Key words:_ State estimation, Model-based deep learning, Attention mechanism, State-space model, Bayesian filter. **1 Introduction** ter (KF) [17], which is an optimal linear quadratic estimator, and has been used in a variety of areas [18–22].
