Flow regimes
Subsonic flow (𝑀∞≤0.8 ) – local Mach number everywhere is less than 1 and hence the flow 
velocity is less than speed of sound everywhere. For airfoils if 𝑀∞≤0.8 the flow field is typically 
considered subsonic 
Transonic (0.8 ≤𝑀∞≤1.2 ) – 𝑀∞ remains subsonic or close to sonic but local 𝑀 can exceed 1 in 
some regions (pockets of local supersonic flow).
Supersonic (𝑀> 1) – local Mach number is more than 1 everywhere
Hypersonic (𝑀∞> 5) – as 𝑀∞ is increased to higher supersonic speeds, the oblique shock wave 
moves closer to the surface and the flow field between the shock and the body becomes very hot.
